Mental Health Resources

Our Mental Health Resources section offers a range of online tools to support your mental health journey and augment your sessions with York Durham Counselling. Additionally, online crisis supports are available 24/7 for those in need of immediate assistance.

Kids Help Phone

24/7 e-mental health service for young people, offering free, confidential support by phone, text, and online resources.

Visit Kids Help Phone

Smiling Mind

A mindfulness app with dozens of guided practices designed by psychologists for both adults and children.

Visit Smiling Mind

9-8-8 Suicide Crisis Helpline

Available 24/7 across Canada to support people thinking about suicide or worried about someone else.

Visit 9-8-8 Helpline

MindShift CBT

A self-help app based on CBT strategies to help manage worry, panic, perfectionism, and social anxiety.

Visit MindShift CBT

Hope for Wellness Helpline

Phone and online chat support available to all Indigenous peoples across Canada, with culturally competent counsellors.

Visit Hope for Wellness

Black Youth Helpline

A multicultural helpline providing support for Black youth, families, and schools through counselling and referrals.

Visit Black Youth Helpline

LGBT Youth Line

Canada’s peer-supported helpline for 2SLGBTQ+ youth, offering free, confidential emotional support by phone, text, chat, and online resources.

Visit LGBT Youth Line

310 Cope

Toronto’s 24/7 emotional support line, providing free, confidential crisis and distress support by phone for individuals experiencing emotional difficulty.

Visit 310 Cope

Anxiety Canada

Canada’s national anxiety support organization, offering free, evidence-based resources, self-help tools, and programs to help individuals understand and manage anxiety.

Visit Anxiety Canada
